Open Source Manifesto
----------------------

On 31 March 2026, I stand as a believer in the power of open source and the spirit of collaboration.
In my daily life, I use tools like Linux, which empower me to learn, create, and innovate freely.
To me, knowledge represents the ability to share knowledge, collaborate without barriers, and grow together as a global community.

I believe that technology should not be limited by restrictions, but instead should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their background.
My vision is to contribute to the world by building an open-source AI learning assistant that helps students learn coding and problem-solving — something meaningful that can help others and be shared openly.

Open source is more than just code; it is a philosophy of trust, transparency, and collective growth.
Through my journey as a developer, I commit to learning continuously, contributing to open communities, and supporting the idea that knowledge should be free and shared.

This is my manifesto — a promise to embrace openness, collaboration, and the true spirit of innovation.
